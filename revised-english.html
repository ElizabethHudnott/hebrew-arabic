  • When two single letter sounds occur next to each other in a way that could be mistaken for a two letter sound then a ' character is inserted to clarify that two one-letter sounds are intended. For example, t'h distinguishes t followed by h from the th sound.
  • I suggest we attempt to undo historical accent changes that have resulted in the /g/ sound becoming dropped from some words like singer in many accents. The older pronunciation is still preserved in Birmingham, Manchester, Liverpool and New York. Under the simplified spelling proposal ng should always be spoken as /ŋg/, at least for those who are learning English for the first time.

Yod-Coalescence

In our simplified spelling convention the combinations dy, sy and ty never occur inside a morpheme. In their place are j, sh and ch. When a y would have historically begun an unstressed syllable then zj coalesces to ʒ.
