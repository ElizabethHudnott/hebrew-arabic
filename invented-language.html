The ElizaLang Language

I made up / am making up my own language!

This article doesn't deal with vocabulary. All words given are either English words or else nonsensical terms like 1es, both of which are used as placeholders for the real ElizaLang words. This article also doesn't deal with the ElizaLang alphabet, punctuation marks or the shapes of the diacritical marks. Presently the letters are identified by their transcriptions into English only. The ElizaLang alphabet is a full alphabet though! That is, the vowels are given their own letters, rather than being diacritic marks like the short vowels in Arabic.

Syllable Structures

The permissable syllable structures are CV, CVV, CVC and CVVC.

Gender

Nouns are not declined by gender and verbs are not conjugated by gender in ElizaLang. Words don't have genders!

Subject & Object: Person, Number & Clusivity

Additionally, the subject can be omitted if the sentence has the same subject as the previous sentence, known as the null subject. The "Unknown" number can also be used in the first person when the number is in fact known but the author wishes to leave it unspecified because the number of people may change over time, such as if additional people subsequently join a team to document ElizaLang, c.f. the Royal "We". An example of an unknown number of inanimate objects is an uncountable noun. The generic person dual refers specifically to the concept of married couples.

Although there are a lot of pronouns, those that share the same column in will share the same suffix and pronouns that appear on the same row will share a common prefix.

Articles

ElizaLang doesn't have any articles. To say, "The X", you just say, "X". To say, "A X", or, "An X", you say, "One X", though of course we haven't introduced the word for "One" yet.

Sentence Structure

ElizaLang is a VSO language.
