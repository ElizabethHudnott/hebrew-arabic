Syllable Structures

The permissable syllable structures are CV, CVV, CVC and CVVC.

Gender

Nouns are not declined by gender and verbs are not conjugated by gender in this language. Words don't have genders!

Subject: Person, Number & Clusivity

Person Singular Dual Plural Unknown
1st person exclusive 1 2 3 4
inclusive – 5 6 7
2nd person 8 9 10 11
3rd person 12 13 14 15
Generic person 16 17 18 –

Additionally, the subject can be omitted if the sentence has the same subject as the previous sentence, the null subject. The "Unknown" case can also be used in the first person when the number is known but the author wishes to leave it unspecified because the number of people may change over time, such as if additional people subsequently join a documentation team, c.f. the royal "We".

Articles

This language doesn't have any articles. To say, "The X", you just say, "X". To say, "A X", or, "An X", you say, "One X", though of course we haven't introduced the word for "One" yet.
