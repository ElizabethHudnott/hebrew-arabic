The Definite Article, The

Hebrew

To take any word, say like, מֶלֶךְ (melekh, "king") and turn it into the definite form ("the king") we attach a prefix onto the beginning of the word. This differs from English where "the" is written as a separate word. The rule basically has two parts, with some exceptional cases that are recorded in .

  1. Add the letter ה to beginning of the word, usually with a patach, הַ.
  2. Add a doubling dagesh to what was originally the first letter of the word, unless that letter is a gutteral.

Hence, for מֶלֶךְ, melekh, we derive the word הַמֶּלֶךְ, hammelekh.

If the first letter originally had a hardening dagesh then the same dot changes its meaning to become a doubling dagesh instead of a hardening one and the pronunciation of the consonant changes. Thus, בַּת (bat, "daughter") becomes הַבַּת (havvat). A dagesh after a vowel is always a doubling dagesh.
